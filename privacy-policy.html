Privacy Policy

How Brinda Clinic collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

Last Updated: January 1, 2025  ·  Effective: January 1, 2025

Brinda Clinic ("we," "us," or "our"), located in Port Blair, Andaman & Nicobar Islands, is committed to protecting the privacy and confidentiality of all patients and website visitors. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our services.

By accessing our website or using our services, you consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with these terms, please do not use our services.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Identification Information

  • Full name, date of birth, gender
  • Contact details: phone number, email address, residential address
  • Government-issued identification numbers (for record purposes)
  • Emergency contact details

Medical and Health Information

  • Medical history, diagnoses, and treatment records
  • Prescription and medication details
  • Laboratory results and diagnostic reports
  • Information provided during appointment bookings and consultations

Automatically Collected Information (Website)

  • IP address, browser type, device information
  • Pages visited, time spent, referring URLs
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies (see Section 7)

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To schedule, manage, and confirm appointments
  • To provide medical consultations and continuity of care
  • To send appointment reminders via SMS or WhatsApp
  • To process insurance claims and billing
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations under Indian law
  • To improve the quality of our services and patient experience
  • To respond to your inquiries and support requests
  • To send health tips, clinic updates, or promotional content (with your consent)

3. Disclosure of Your Information

Brinda Clinic does not sell, trade, or rent your personal or medical information to third parties. We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

  • Referral care: When referring you to a specialist or secondary care facility, relevant medical information will be shared with the receiving provider.
  • Legal obligation: When required by law, court order, or government regulations (e.g., notifiable disease reporting).
  • Insurance processing: With your insurer or third-party administrator for the purpose of claims processing, with your authorisation.
  • Service providers: Trusted technology vendors who assist in operating our website or communication systems, bound by strict confidentiality agreements.
  • Emergency situations: With emergency medical personnel or family members when necessary to protect your life or health.

4. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect your information from unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encrypted storage for digital medical records
  • Password-protected systems with restricted staff access
  • HTTPS/SSL encryption for all website data transmission
  • Regular security audits and staff training on data privacy
  • Physical security controls for paper-based medical records

While we strive to protect your data, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but will notify you in the event of a significant data breach as required under applicable law.

5. Retention of Data

Medical records are retained for a minimum of 7 years from the date of last consultation, in compliance with the Medical Council of India guidelines and applicable state regulations. After this period, records are securely destroyed. Website usage data and non-medical personal data are retained for no more than 2 years.

6. Your Rights

As a patient or website user, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to Access: You may request a copy of your personal and medical records held by us.
  • Right to Correction: You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Right to Withdrawal of Consent: You may withdraw consent for marketing communications at any time.
  • Right to Portability: You may request your medical records in a portable format for transfer to another provider.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at care@brindaclinic.in or visit the clinic in person with valid ID.

7. Cookies

Our website uses cookies to enhance your browsing experience. Cookies are small text files stored on your device. We use:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the website to function (theme preference, session management).
  • Analytics cookies: Used to understand how visitors use our website (we may use Google Analytics or similar). These are anonymised and aggregated.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect some website functionality.

8. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (such as Google Maps or social media platforms). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those websites and encourage you to review their respective privacy policies.

9. Children's Privacy

Brinda Clinic provides medical care to patients of all ages, including minors. For patients under the age of 18, we obtain consent from a parent or legal guardian before collecting or processing personal information, except in medical emergencies. Parents or guardians may request access to or deletion of their child's records by contacting us directly.

10. Changes to This Policy

We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy at any time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our services after changes constitute acceptance of the updated policy.
